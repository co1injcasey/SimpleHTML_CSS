Cuba-Cities

Havana

Havana is the capital of cuba. It is also its largest city with a population of 2.1 million. It was founded in the 16th century by the Spanish. Havana became a city in 1592 which was proclaimed a city by King Phillip the second of Spain.When the city was first established it was a leader in world trade. Trading not only incredible amounts of sugar, but also a leader in the participation of slave trade. These trades made the new city into a flourishing economy and a great trade center for the new world. Today the city faces poverty and has had decades of economic downfall. Almost 10 percent of the citys current population lives in poor conditions.

Guantanamo

Guantanamo is a city in southeast cuba. Over 200,000 people call this city home, and its primary occupations include the production of sugar as well as coffee. Another product this area produces is cotton wool. The city was founded in 1797 and is 286 square miles.It is home to Guantanamo Bay, a very large naval base on the south side of the island of Cuba. It is the largest harbor on the south side of the island. The United States also has a Detention camp in Guantamo to hold extra dangerous prisoners.It is an extremly well gaurded prison institution.
